North Wales Legends

An adventure Holiday experiencing the finest of everything Welsh like never before.

The Welsh Legends Trail is a fascinating and unforgettable trip into the World of Celtic Bygone legends, married up with unique accommodation and great welsh food.

Transported through the beautiful rugged Hills of Wales and taken to places of extreme historical importance, where fascinating stories will be told of what was and now forgotten.

  • Day one

    • Ruthin Castle

      Ruthin Castle is a beautiful retreat; steeped in history and nestled in acres of parkland beside the Clwydian Range in North Wales. Here you can indulge yourself with exquisite dining, unwind in our distinctive spa or revel in one of our renowned Medieval Feasts before sinking into a luxurious bed for a sleep worthy of a Prince or Princess.

    • Dining

      Ruthin Castle provides a superb stage for hosting themed medieval dinners modeled upon the legendary feasts the Earl of Warwick held at The Castle in the 16th century.

      Revellers assemble in the Presence Chamber for the partaking of bread and salt, age-old symbols of hospitality. The fully costumed Court Steward will then escort you into the lofty Banqueting Hall where the Ladies of the Court will entertain, sing (in both Welsh and English), jest and lead the evening’s feasting…

  • Day two

    • Gladstone Library

      This institution pays tribute to William Gladstone. It is Britain's finest residential library, and its only Prime Ministerial library. It was founded by the great Victorian statesman himself and, following his death in 1898, became the national memorial to his life and work as well as one of the few Grade I-listed buildings in North Wales.

    • Accomodation & Dining

      Dining will be at the beautiful hotel restaurant. You could be sampling fresh fare from the surrounding grounds, or contemporary takes on regional specialities.

  • Day three

    • Daniel Owen

      Owen is the earliest Welsh-language novelist whose works are still widely read. He is credited with starting the tradition of the Welsh language. He was an influence on many later novelists, such as Kate Roberts and T. Rowland Hughes.

    • Accomodation & Dining

      Soughton is a luxury hotel, wedding and event venue. On the border of Cheshire and North Wales, this stunning Georgian country house, approached down a sweeping driveway and surrounded by landscaped gardens has recently been lovingly refurbished. Contempory in feel, yet full of character, charm and warmth, the Hall boasts 14 individually decorated bedrooms, an award winning restaurant and a relaxing alternative to the hustle and bustle of city hotels.

  • Day three

    • Moel Famau

      Moel Famau (or Moel Fama) is the highest hill within the Clwydian Range. It is also surrounded by several well-preserved Iron-Age hill forts. A northern part of the Offa's Dyke footpath, one of the UK's most popular National Trails, crosses the summit of Moel Famau and the Jubilee Tower.

    • Accomodation & Dining

      If you love well presented delicious locally sourced food then this is for you, one of the best kept secrets in Wales
